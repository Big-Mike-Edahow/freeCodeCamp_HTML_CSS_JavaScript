Trucking Adventures

Gear Up

When a trucker is going to spend weeks on end out on the road, the proper gear is essential. A sleeping back, sheets, and pillows and pillowcases can make the sleeper bunk of a truck feel like home. A hard hat, safety vest, steel toed boots, and leather gloves keep a driver safe, and allow them to access job sites where personal protection equipment is required. Bottled water by the case is a must, as it's too expensive to buy water by the bottle a truckstop.

Handy Tools

  • Cutting Knife
  • Tire Air Guage
  • Hammer to Check Tires
  • Flashlight
  • Leatherman Multi-tool
  • 20ft Jumper Cables
  • 50ft Air Hose

Considerations

Air fresheners will give the truck that new car smell. Armorall Wipes will help protect the dash and give it a glossy shine. A bottle of Windex and some paper towels to clean windows will reduce sun glare.
